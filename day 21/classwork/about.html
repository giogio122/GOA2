Cristiano Ronaldo

Over the course of his career, Portuguese footballer Cristiano Ronaldo has received five Ballon d'Or/FIFA Ballon d'Or awards,[a] the most for a European player. Widely regarded as one of the greatest players of all time, Ronaldo holds the record for most goals in the UEFA Champions League (140 goals), and the record for most goals in the UEFA European Championship (14), its qualification stage (40), and the FIFA Club World Cup (7), as well as most goals scored in a UEFA Champions League season (17 in 2013–14), most international goals (143), and most appearances in a national team (226). He has scored a record 953 senior career goals for club and country. Moreover, he is one of only three players to have made over 1,300 professional career appearances.

Messi

Lionel Andrés "Leo" Messi[note 1] (Spanish: [ljoˈnel anˈdɾes ˈmesi] ⓘ; born 24 June 1987) is an Argentine professional footballer who plays as a forward for and captains both Major League Soccer club Inter Miami and the Argentina national team. Widely regarded as one of the greatest players in history, Messi has set numerous records for individual accolades won throughout his professional footballing career, including eight Ballon d'Ors, six European Golden Shoes, and eight times being named the world's best player by FIFA.[note 2] In 2025, he was named the All Time Men's World Best Player by the IFFHS. He is the most decorated player in the history of professional football having won 45 team trophies.[note 3] Messi's records include most goals in a calendar year (91), most goals for a single club (672 for Barcelona), most goals in La Liga (474), most assists in international football (61), most goal contributions in the FIFA World Cup (21), and most goal contributions in the Copa América (32). A prolific goalscorer and creative playmaker, Messi has scored over 890 senior career goals and provided over 400 assists for club and country—the most of any player—resulting in over 1,290 goal contributions, the highest total in the sport's history.[21]

Pele

Pelé's statistics include a career total of over 1,200 goals, with approximately 77 goals in 92 games for the Brazilian national team and 643 goals in 659 games for his club Santos. While his official goal tally is debated, with sources ranging from 778 in official matches to a Guinness World Record of 1,279 total goals, his overall impact is undisputed. He also won three World Cups with Brazil, was a three-time World Cup champion, and played professionally from 1956 to 1977.

Zidane

Zinédine Zidane's career statistics include approximately 690 games, 125 goals, and 142 assists across all clubs, with his most appearances made for Real Madrid. Nationally, he played 108 games for France, scoring 31 goals. His playing career spanned 17 seasons and included major successes such as winning the Ballon d'Or in 1998.
